To put the steps for "paw" training in order, the correct sequence is:

1. **Put treats in front of the dog's nose** (Given as Step 1)
2. **Add a verbal cue** (This helps the dog associate a command with the action)
3. **Wait with your hand out** (This encourages the dog to paw at your hand)
4. **Try again with treats in front of the dog's nose and repeat "good" each time the dog gives the paw correctly** (Given as Step 4)
5. **Grab the dog’s paw if he/she doesn’t give it to you** (To reinforce the action if the dog doesn’t respond)
6. **Say "good" when the dog moves either paw** (Positive reinforcement for any movement)

Two steps are not needed:  
- "Say 'NO' firmly if the dog isn’t paying attention" (not suitable for positive reinforcement training)
- "Take treats away, rely only on hand gestures" (not necessary at this stage of training)
