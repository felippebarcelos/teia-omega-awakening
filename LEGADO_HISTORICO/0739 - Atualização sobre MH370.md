In addition, scientists have been analyzing barnacles found on a piece of the aircraft's flaperon that washed ashore on Reunion Island in 2015. These studies aim to determine the likely drift patterns and original location of the wreckage.

Ocean Infinity, a private company, has expressed interest in resuming the search on a "no find, no fee" basis, which indicates a potential for renewed efforts to locate the missing plane soon.
