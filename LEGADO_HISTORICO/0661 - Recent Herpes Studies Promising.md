quais são os estudos mais recentes promissores em relação a herpes

---

### ASSISTENTE

Recent studies on herpes have shown promising advancements in both treatment and potential cures. One of the most notable developments is the experimental gene therapy from the Fred Hutchinson Cancer Center, which uses a meganuclease enzyme to cut the herpes virus DNA, significantly reducing the virus's ability to replicate. This approach has shown to eliminate up to 90% of the herpes simplex virus 1 (HSV-1) in preclinical studies with mice, demonstrating a strong potential for human application in the future【9†source】.

Another exciting advancement is the clinical trials for Pritelivir, a new class of antiviral drug that targets the viral helicase-primase complex, inhibiting the virus's replication. Pritelivir has shown promising results in phase three clinical trials, particularly among immunocompromised patients, and is now being tested in healthy individuals. This drug could potentially become a significant option for managing herpes infections more effectively【7†source】.

In the realm of vaccines, a novel trivalent vaccine developed by researchers at the University of Pennsylvania has achieved nearly 100% efficacy in animal models. This vaccine targets HSV-2, stimulating multiple antibodies to block the virus from entering cells and neutralizing its immune-evading strategies. These results are highly promising and indicate that human trials could be the next step【8†source】.

These studies represent significant strides towards more effective treatments and potential cures for herpes, offering hope to millions affected by the virus worldwide.
